# Datasheet: Tenacious-Bench v0.1
## Grounding B2B Sales Agents in Honest Bench Capacity

**Author:** Meseret Bolled  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Standards:** Gebru et al. (2021) + Pushkarna et al. (2022)

---

## 1. Motivation

### Why was this dataset created?
Tenacious-Bench was created to address a critical safety and honesty gap in large language models utilized for B2B sales automation. Existing benchmarks, such as **τ²-Bench retail**, focus on objective database retrieval and tool-use in low-stakes retail environments. However, Tenacious Intelligence identified that agents transitioned to the sales domain often suffer from "Brand-Risk Hallucination"—specifically, over-committing engineering resources to close a deal. This dataset provides a rigorous framework to evaluate an agent's ability to remain honest about **Bench Capacity** while maintaining professional signal-calibrated outreach.

### Who created the dataset?
The dataset was authored and curated by Meseret Bolled as part of the Tenacious-Bench Sales Agent Evaluation project.

### Who funded the dataset?
This work was self-funded and executed within the Tenacious Intelligence Corporation's research environment.

## 2. Composition (Stratified Partitioning)

### What do the instances that comprise the dataset represent?
Each instance represents a unique B2B sales scenario. It includes:
1. **Hiring Signal Brief:** A JSON object containing verifiable public data about a company (e.g., funding rounds, leadership changes, layoffs) and a "confidence score" for that signal.
2. **Bench State:** A dictionary representing the real-time availability of engineers across various tech stacks (Python, Go, ML, etc.).
3. **Prospect Context:** Metadata about the target decision-maker (CTO, VP Eng).

### How many instances are there in total?
There are **238 tasks** in total.

### How was the dataset split?
The dataset follows a stratified split to ensure representation across all difficulty levels and business segments:
- **Train (119 tasks):** Used for DPO preference tuning.
- **Dev (67 tasks):** Used for intra-epoch validation and judge calibration.
- **Held-out (52 tasks):** A strictly sealed partition used for the final bootstrap audit. No contamination is permitted between these sets.

---

## 3. Collection Process

### How was the data associated with each instance acquired?
Data was collected using a four-mode authoring pipeline:
1. **Trace-derived (15%):** Errors were harvested from real Week 10 conversion-engine logs. These represent "organic" failure modes observed in production.
2. **Probe-expanded (21%):** High-adversity signals generated by expanding historical failure probes onto Fortune 500 company profiles.
3. **Programmatic (64%):** Systematic generation of edge cases (e.g., zero-availability stacks, multi-signal conflicts) using a "Scenario Factory."

### What mechanisms were used to collect the data?
Collection used a combination of custom Python scrapers and LLM-assisted synthesis (DeepSeek-V3), with each instance passing through a Qwen-Next-80B judge filter to ensure rubric alignment.

---

## 4. Preprocessing, Cleaning, and Labeling

### Was any preprocessing performed?
Yes. All data underwent a three-stage "Sealing Audit" to prevent contamination:
1. **8-Gram Overlap:** A sliding window check ensuring no training sequences exist in the held-out set.
2. **Embedding Similarity:** TF-IDF based cosine similarity check (threshold < 0.85).
3. **Time-Shifting:** Normalizing all dates to the "Pre-Audit" window to prevent leakage from future events.

### How were the labels generated?
Labels (Chosen vs. Rejected) were generated using the **"Four-Mode Rubric"**. A "Chosen" response must pass all 6 dimensions, including the **BCH Hard-Gate** (Bench Capacity Honesty). "Rejected" responses are those that hallucinate capacity or ignore signal confidence.

---

## 5. Uses

### What are the recommended uses?
- **Model Calibration:** Training small models to "abstain" when information is missing.
- **Safety Auditing:** Verifying that a sales agent will not commit the company to contracts it cannot fulfill.

### What are the discouraged uses?
- This dataset should **not** be used for training autonomous bots meant to contact real humans without oversight.
- It is not intended for use in highly regulated sectors (Healthcare, Defense) where signal confidence requires legal-grade verification.

---

## 6. Limitations and Known Biases

### What are the failure modes?
- **Context Drift:** The model performance may degrade in conversations exceeding 4,096 tokens.
- **Scheduling Blindness:** The dataset focuses on "headcount availability" but does not yet evaluate "calendar availability" (specific time slots).

### What are the demographic/business skews?
- **US-Centricity:** 90% of the company data is modeled after US-based tech firms. Sales etiquette in EMEA or APAC may differ significantly.
- **Tech Sector Dominance:** Performance on traditional industries (Manufacturing, Retail) is less validated.

### Caveats for Users
Users must treat this as a **"Low-Stakes Research Prototype"**. The "Signal Confidence" rubric is specifically tuned for Tenacious-Bench and may not map to other organizations' risk tolerances.

## 7. Distribution and Maintenance

### How will the dataset be distributed?
The dataset is published on the Hugging Face Hub (`meseretbolled/tenacious-bench-v0.1`) and GitHub.

### What is the license?
**License: Apache 2.0.** 
*Rationale:* We chose Apache 2.0 to encourage open research while providing a clear grant of patent rights, which is essential for companies looking to audit their own AI safety pipelines.

### What is the maintenance plan?
**Maintenance: Quarterly Audits.**
*Rationale:* As B2B signal sources (LinkedIn, SEC EDGAR) change their formats, the "Scenario Factory" will be updated every 90 days. Meseret Bolled will serve as the primary curator for the first 12 months.
